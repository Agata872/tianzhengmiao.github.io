GNN-based Precoding in Cell-free Massive MIMO

Cell-free massive MIMO aims to provide uniform high-quality wireless service via distributed Access Points (APs). This project proposes a GNN-based precoding framework trained on synthetic CSI and fine-tuned using real-world measurements from our Techtile testbed.

System Overview

Figure: System model of the Cell-free Massive MIMO network with distributed APs.

GNN Architecture

The GNN operates on a bipartite graph, where APs and UEs are nodes, and edges represent wireless links characterized by CSI.

Real-world Dataset

Real-world CSI data was collected from a testbed with 33 APs mounted on a ceiling. 500 spatial positions were sampled to create diverse user combinations for training and testing.
