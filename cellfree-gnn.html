GNN-based Precoding in Cell-free Massive MIMO

Figure: System model of the Cell-free Massive MIMO network with distributed APs.

Background & Motivation

Cell-free massive MIMO aims to provide uniform high-quality wireless service via distributed Access Points (APs). However, designing scalable and robust precoding under real-world channel conditions remains a significant challenge. We address this by using Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), which learn mappings from channel states to precoding weights.

Our Approach

  • Pretraining: A GNN is trained using synthetic CSI based on Rayleigh fading and 3GPP models.
  • Fine-tuning: The pretrained GNN is adapted to real-world CSI data collected from our Techtile testbed.
  • Transfer learning: We apply a layer-freezing technique to preserve knowledge while adapting to new conditions.

Figure: GNN operates over an edge-centric bipartite graph, modeling AP-UE channel links.

Techtile Testbed

The real-world CSI is collected in a physical indoor environment with 33 distributed APs on the ceiling and a single UE moving across 500 spatial positions. We construct 2-user and 4-user datasets from these single-user measurements to simulate multi-user scenarios.

Key Results

  • After fine-tuning, our GNN achieves a 15.7% sum-rate gain on real data compared to the pretrained model.
  • It outperforms conventional precoding schemes like Conjugate Beamforming (CB) and approaches the performance of Zero Forcing (ZF).
  • Our layer-freezing strategy (freezing 4 of 8 layers) achieves the best transfer result.
